Plastic Injection Mold

Project in collaboration with PSI plastics.

In my final semester of college (Jan 2023 - Jun 2023) I led a team of four in a collaborative project with PSI Plastics to design a self-watering flower pot optimized for plastic injection molding.

We began by modeling the flower pot and its mold in SolidWorks, carefully balancing aesthetics with manufacturability and functionality We determined the optimal draft angles to preserve the original design while minimizing risks during injection. PSI Plastics provided us with professional guidelines and tolerances, which we implemented to ensure material efficiency and mold reliability

Although budget and time constraints prevented full-scale mold fabrication, we produced a 3D-printed prototype that replicated all critical features, including cavities, angles, and draft surfaces as if it were ready for injection. We also printed a physical flower pot to demonstrate fit and function.

The final prototype was presented to PSI Plastics, our faculty, and showcased at a university science exposition. PSI Plastics confirmed the mold was fully functional and could be manufactured if needed, this was a rewarding validation of our design and engineering process.

This project was a hands-on dive into CAD modeling, CAM programming, mold simulation, and industry collaboration. It taught me how to lead a team through technical challenges, apply real-world standards, and deliver a validated prototype under constraints.

Flower Pot Composition

the challenge was to create a manufacturable product composed of two interlocking components: a base to hold the plant and a lower reservoir for passive hydration.

Simulations

The injection mold fabrication was simulated through CAM in Fusion360. Also, thermal and flow analyses were performed in Altair and Ansys respectively to study plastic behavior during the injection proces.
